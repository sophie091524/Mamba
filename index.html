Mamba Meets Neural Audio Codecs: A Generative Approach to Speech Enhancement

Anonymous Author(s)

Improving speech quality in noisy and reverberant environments is important for speech processing tasks. Recently, Mamba, a state-space model incorporating a selection mechanism, has achieved promising performance on automatic speech recognition and speech separation. Unlike Transformer, whose self-attention mechanism has a computational complexity that grows quadratically with sequence length, input-dependent selection mechanism of Mamba achieves linear complexity, making it an alternative for Transformer. In this paper, we apply Mamba for speech enhancement from a generative perspective, similar to language models, where Mamba generates discrete code tokens corresponding to clean speech but from the degraded intput. Experimental results on the DNS Challenge test sets show superior DNSMOS scores for both simulated and real recordings.
